# Heights of Eldorado

**Member, Eldorado Hotels and Resorts**
Lagos–Calabar Coastal Highway, Uyo, Akwa Ibom State, Nigeria

## 1. Brand Story and Positioning

### Brand Story

Heights of Eldorado rises where the Lagos–Calabar Coastal Highway meets the green heart of Akwa Ibom — the Land of Promise. For centuries, this coastline drew traders, navigators, and seekers across the Gulf of Guinea; the legend of Eldorado, the golden city just beyond the horizon, found its truest expression not in myth but in places like this — where palm groves shimmer gold at dusk, where the estuaries of the Qua Iboe run toward the Atlantic, and where hospitality is a birthright, not a business. Heights of Eldorado is built on the conviction that the golden city was never lost. It is here, in Uyo, expressed through Ibibio warmth, coastal serenity, and craftsmanship gathered from two continents.

The hotel stands as the flagship expression of Eldorado Hotels and Resorts: a modern classic tower and garden estate where international five-star discipline meets the artistry of southern Nigeria — raffia masters from Ikot Ekpene, bronze casters and weavers from across the Niger Delta and beyond, and precision joinery and stonework sourced through Foshan and Guangzhou. Every arrival is an ascent: from the new coastal highway into a realm of quiet gold, deep green, and unhurried grace. Guests do not merely stay at Heights of Eldorado; they are received into it.

### Brand Pillars

1. **Elevated Quiet Luxury** — Wealth whispered, never shouted. Champagne-gold accents, honed stone, and deep upholstery replace glitter and gloss. The luxury is in the silence of the corridors, the weight of the door, the temperature of the towel.
2. **The Land of Promise, Curated** — Akwa Ibom's landscape and culture — raffia, palm, estuary, nsibidi-inspired geometry, Ibibio hospitality — interpreted at gallery standard throughout art, cuisine, and ritual. Local character as connoisseurship, not decoration.
3. **Two-Continent Craftsmanship** — A deliberate China–Nigeria supply and design axis: Foshan-fabricated joinery, stone, and lighting engineered to Nigerian climate and creative direction, finished and layered with Nigerian art and textiles. World-class execution, unmistakably local soul.
4. **Anticipatory Grace** — Service modeled on the world's great luxury houses: staff empowered to resolve, personalize, and delight without escalation. Every guest known by name by the second encounter.
5. **Resilient Serenity** — Security, power, water, and connectivity engineered to be invisible and unconditional. The guest experiences only calm; the infrastructure absorbs the volatility.

### Brand Promise

*At Heights of Eldorado, every guest ascends into a world of golden calm — where the Land of Promise keeps its promise, personally, to you.*

## 2. Guest Experience Vision

### The Arrival Sequence

From the Lagos–Calabar Coastal Highway, the hotel announces itself with restraint: a 400-metre approach avenue lined with royal palms and low, warm-white landscape lighting, terminating in a gatehouse of charcoal granite and bronze. Security screening is discreet and swift — a porte cochère beyond, not a checkpoint. The porte cochère itself is a double-height pavilion: a coffered hardwood ceiling (iroko, oiled, fabricated in Foshan to local species-match), a floor of flamed grey granite inset with brass strips radiating like sunrays, and a suspended sculptural chandelier of hand-woven raffia and antique brass commissioned from Ikot Ekpene weavers.

Doormen in tailored charcoal linen with gold-thread lapel detail open doors in synchrony. The guest crosses a threshold of book-matched Calacatta-vein marble into a lobby that smells faintly of the house scent — pressed palm blossom, cedar, and a trace of citrus and sea salt, referencing the Ibeno coastline an hour east. Sound is controlled: a live kora or ekpo-inspired ambient composition at low volume, ceiling acoustics tuned so conversation dissolves at three metres. Lighting sits at 2700K, layered — a glowing onyx reception desk, picture lights on a commissioned art wall, and a 12-metre atrium void above, crowned by a skylight screened in a brass nsibidi-derived lattice that throws slow-moving patterned shadows across the floor through the afternoon.

There is no queueing. Arriving guests are met by name (flight manifests and pre-arrival profiles are worked in advance), seated in the welcome salon, and offered the signature arrival ritual: a chilled hibiscus-and-calamansi cordial served in hand-blown amber glass, with a warm towel scented with lemongrass. Registration happens tableside on a tablet. Luggage reaches the room before the guest does.

### The In-Room Experience

**Typical guest room (Deluxe):** The door closes with a soft pneumatic hush. Ahead, floor-to-ceiling glazing frames Uyo's green canopy rolling toward the coastal plain — from the upper floors, on clear days, a silver line of the Atlantic. The palette is warm ivory, palm-green, and muted gold. Underfoot: wide-plank engineered oak with a hand-knotted rug in an abstracted raffia weave pattern. The bed — a custom 2m × 2m platform with an upholstered wall panel in ribbed bouclé framed in fluted walnut — carries 400-thread-count Egyptian cotton, a ten-layer mattress build, and a pillow menu of six. Bedside panels control lighting scenes, curtains, and climate; one touch on "Goodnight" closes the sheers, drops the room to 40% light, and arms the night path lighting. The bathroom is a room in itself: honed travertine, twin vanities in fluted glass and brass, a freestanding oval tub facing the view, rain and handheld shower, and amenities in refillable ceramic vessels made by a Calabar–Uyo pottery collective.

**Flagship suite (The Eldorado Suite):** A corner residence on the top floors with a 270-degree sweep — sunset over the palm belt to the west, the highway's ribbon of light to the south, the estuary haze to the east. Interiors move from the guest-room palette into deeper registers: smoked eucalyptus veneer walls, a champagne-gold bar with backlit honey onyx, a dining room for eight beneath a raffia-and-crystal chandelier, and a master bath in book-matched Panda White marble with a steam shower and a soaking tub carved from a single block of granite. Original art throughout — commissioned works by Akwa Ibom and Cross River artists, each documented in a leather-bound suite catalogue. A dedicated suite attendant, private check-in, and a pantry pre-stocked to the guest's profile complete the experience.

### Service Philosophy

Service at Heights of Eldorado follows three disciplines:

- **Known, not processed.** A single guest-profile system feeds every department. Preferences captured once — pillow firmness, coffee order, allergy, preferred newspaper, prayer times — appear everywhere, forever, across all Eldorado Hotels and Resorts properties.
- **Empowered to resolve.** Every colleague, from housekeeping to concierge, carries discretionary authority (a defined naira envelope per incident) to fix a problem or create a moment of delight on the spot, without seeking approval. Incidents are logged for learning, never for blame.
- **Signature touches.** The Eldorado brand ritual set includes: the golden-hour turndown (a small gilded box with a rotating Akwa Ibom delicacy — coconut candy, tiger-nut brittle — and a card explaining its origin); the departure blessing (a raffia-wrapped bottle of house-blended zobo or cold-pressed palm-fruit juice for the road); and the "Second Welcome" — returning guests find their room preset to their previous stay's exact preferences, with a handwritten note referencing their last visit.

## 4. Interior Design Language

**Overall style:** *Modern classic coastal — West African artistry within a disciplined international luxury frame, executed with Chinese precision fabrication.* Symmetry, generous proportions, and classical rhythm in the architecture; contemporary Nigerian art, raffia craft, and nsibidi-inspired geometry in the layer that touches the guest.

### Color Palette

- **Public spaces:** Primary — warm ivory and bone (walls, stone); Secondary — deep palm green and smoked walnut; Accents — champagne gold, aged brass, and a controlled note of estuary blue-grey.
- **Guest rooms:** Primary — ivory, oat, and greige; Secondary — muted palm and clay; Accents — brushed gold hardware, amber glass, and one artwork-driven color moment per room.
- **Rooftop (Àkàrà & Fire):** Charcoal, ember-orange, and black granite with brass — the single space permitted a darker, hotter register.

### Key Material Combinations

- **Flooring:** Book-matched light marble with brass inlay strips (lobby); smoked-oak herringbone (lounge); terrazzo with shell aggregate (all-day dining); wide-plank engineered oak over acoustic underlay (rooms); honed travertine (spa, bathrooms).
- **Walls:** Venetian plaster in ivory; fluted walnut and eucalyptus veneer panelling; upholstered acoustic panels in bouclé and raw silk; feature walls in hand-applied raffia weave panels behind protective glass.
- **Ceilings:** Coffered iroko-tone timber (arrival), acoustic plaster with concealed cove lighting (most spaces), charred-timber slats (rooftop).
- **Joinery & metals:** Fluted and reeded timber profiles throughout; all visible metal in brushed brass or bronze — never chrome; shop drawings and fabrication via Foshan partners with Nigerian-climate specifications (humidity-stable engineered cores, marine-grade finishes).
- **Textiles:** Belgian linen sheers, blackout in woven jacquard, velvet and full-grain leather seating, hand-knotted rugs in abstracted raffia and wave patterns (custom-loomed, designed in Nigeria, produced in Guangzhou mills).

### Signature Motifs (repeatable, fabricable)

1. **The Eldorado Sunburst** — a radiating brass-line motif (floor inlays, lift lobby medallions, stationery) abstracting sunrise over the Atlantic coast.
2. **Nsibidi Lattice** — a geometric screen pattern derived respectfully from nsibidi ideographic forms of the Cross River region; used in bronze atrium screens, balustrades, and door hardware.
3. **Raffia Weave** — the Ikot Ekpene weave translated across scales: chandeliers, wall panels, rug patterns, amenity packaging, key-card sleeves.
4. **The Golden Band** — a single continuous brass datum line at 900mm running through public-space wall treatments, unifying the journey from porte cochère to ballroom.

These four motifs, with the palette and materials above, constitute the full sourcing brief an interior contractor in Foshan can price and prototype from.

## 5. Room and Suite Design

### Deluxe Room — 42 m² (52 keys)

- **Layout:** Entry gallery with full-height wardrobe (interior-lit, one mirrored door); bathroom to one side of entry; bedroom opens to full-width glazing. King bed centered on upholstered feature wall; chaise-and-table seating at the window; floating desk-console combination in walnut along the side wall (doubles as luggage bench and minibar housing).
- **Lighting:** Four layers — cove ambient (2700K, dimmable), bedside reading spots (adjustable, 3000K focused), art accent lights, and motion-activated floor-level night path from bed to bathroom. Preset scenes: Arrival, Relax, Work, Goodnight.
- **Tech:** Bedside and entry touch panels (lighting, curtains, climate, privacy/MUR); inverter VRF climate with occupancy setback; motorised sheers and blackout; 55" concealed-frame TV with casting; international + USB-C outlets at every surface; silent minibar.
- **Materials & color:** Ivory plaster walls, oak floor with raffia-pattern rug, bouclé bedhead in oat framed in fluted walnut, brushed-gold hardware, travertine bathroom with walk-in rain shower, single vanity in stone with brass frame.

### Executive Room — 55 m² (22 keys; executive floors 9–11)

- **Layout:** Distinct sleep and work/lounge zones divided by a fluted-glass-and-walnut partial screen. Full executive desk with task chair facing the view; two-seat sofa and lounge chair; wardrobe upgraded to walk-in dressing niche. Bathroom adds freestanding tub beside the shower, twin vanities.
- **Lighting:** Deluxe scheme plus dedicated desk task lighting (4000K switchable for video calls) and tub-side dimming scene.
- **Tech:** Deluxe package plus in-room tablet with concierge chat and F&B ordering; video-call lighting preset; espresso machine; digital laundry pickup.
- **Materials & color:** Palette deepens — greige plaster, smoked-oak floor, palm-green velvet lounge chair, clay-toned rug, honed travertine bath with a bronze-framed feature mirror. Access to Executive Lounge (floor 11) included.
- **Coastal Suite variant — 78 m² (12 keys):** Same language with separated living room, guest WC, and a private balcony oriented toward the coastal plain; estuary blue-grey accents.

### The Eldorado Suite — 160 m² (3 keys, floors 11–12)

- **Layout:** Entry foyer with art niche; living room with dining for eight and service pantry; corner master with 270° glazing; dressing room; master bath with steam shower, twin stone vanities, and monolithic granite tub; guest powder room; connectable adjacent Deluxe for staff/family.
- **Lighting:** Fully scened per zone; chandelier over dining (raffia and crystal); picture lights on all commissioned art; circadian tuning in the master (warmer and lower after 21:00).
- **Tech:** Whole-suite automation with a single "Host" scene for entertaining; integrated AV in living room; touch panels in every zone plus in-suite tablet; discreet CCTV-free privacy design with corridor presence sensor for the suite attendant.
- **Materials & color:** Smoked eucalyptus panelling, champagne-gold bar with backlit honey onyx, Panda White book-matched master bath, silk-and-wool rugs, full original-art program with catalogue.

## 6. F&B Concept

### Ibom Terrace — All-Day Restaurant

A garden pavilion off the lobby serving breakfast, lunch, and dinner: an international brasserie foundation (continental breakfast theatre, wood-fired flatbreads, grills, plant-forward bowls) interwoven with a serious Akwa Ibom kitchen — afang and edikang ikong elevated with tableside finishing, ekpang nkukwo as a signature lunch, fisherman's soup with estuary catch, native pepper soups, and a Sunday "Land of Promise" brunch that becomes Uyo's social institution. Atmosphere: bright terrazzo, cane and linen, doors opening to a herb-garden terrace. Deliberately porous to the city — Uyo's professional class, government and oil-sector visitors, and diaspora returnees should treat it as their canteen of choice, with a dedicated entrance from the garden so locals never feel like they are crossing a hotel lobby to dine.

### Àkàrà & Fire — Rooftop Destination Restaurant & Bar

Uyo's new landmark: a 12th-floor open-fire grill and bar where everything touches flame — suya-spiced dry-aged beef, whole grilled estuary fish, charred plantain with smoked-pepper butter, and the namesake àkàrà served as a refined amuse-bouche with three heritage sauces. The bar leads with palm and gold: the **Golden Hour** (aged rum, palm syrup, calamansi, edible gold leaf) and a zero-proof zobo-and-smoke ritual serve. Design: charred timber, black granite, ember lighting, a patinated-brass open kitchen as the stage, and a west-facing teak deck built for the sunset over the palm belt. Music: live highlife reinterpreted through a jazz trio early, a measured Afro-soul DJ set late. Reservation-led, with a strict acoustic and dress standard that keeps it destination, not nightclub.

### Service and Presentation Standard

Service across all outlets follows luxury-house discipline: synchronized plate service at tables of four or more, staff trained to read the table (business pace vs. celebration pace), no interrupted conversations, and every menu item traceable to a named farm, boat, or maker — the estuary fisherman, the Itu farm, the Ikot Ekpene weaver who made the bread basket. Presentation carries the brand motifs: the Golden Band as a brass rim detail on charger plates, raffia-woven menu covers, and the Eldorado Sunburst debossed on every check presenter.

## 7. Operational and Sustainability Considerations

### Energy Resilience and Sustainability

- **Target: 60–70% of annual electrical load from solar-plus-storage.** Rooftop and carport photovoltaic arrays (podium and parking canopies) paired with a containerized lithium battery plant; synchronized dual generators retained for backup only, with a design goal of near-zero diesel hours in normal operation.
- **Demand-side discipline:** inverter VRF air conditioning with occupancy setback throughout; 100% LED with daylight and presence dimming; heat-pump water heating with solar thermal pre-heat; variable-speed pumps for pool and domestic water; high-performance glazing and deep balcony shading on west elevations to cut cooling load at source.
- **Water and waste:** borehole with full treatment plant and bottled-water-elimination program (in-house still/sparkling bottling); rainwater harvesting for irrigation; on-site organic waste composting feeding the kitchen garden.

### Security and Guest Comfort (Nigeria-Specific)

- Layered, low-visibility security: single controlled vehicular entry with under-vehicle screening at the gatehouse, hostile-vehicle landscape mitigation (bollards disguised as planters and water features), CCTV with analytics, and vetted, professionally trained in-house security in plain tailored uniform — the guest reads hospitality, not fortification.
- Coordination protocols with state security services and the airport (Victor Attah International, ~20 minutes) for VIP movements; secure ground transport fleet with trained chauffeurs as a standard guest service on the new highway corridor.
- Redundant everything the guest can feel: dual-path internet (fibre + microwave/LEO backup), 24/7 power and water as an absolute brand guarantee, and an in-house clinic with a doctor on call.

### Staff Culture and Training

- A pre-opening **Eldorado Academy** (12 weeks) covering luxury service choreography, storytelling of Akwa Ibom heritage, and empowerment protocols; ongoing daily line-ups where one guest story is shared and one standard rehearsed.
- Recruit for warmth, train for skill: prioritize Akwa Ibom hires (target 70%+ local employment) with career pathways across the Eldorado Hotels and Resorts group; expatriate specialists in culinary and spa leadership paired with local deputies on defined succession timelines.
- Staff facilities finished to near-guest standard — the brand's internal promise is that colleagues are treated as the first guests.
